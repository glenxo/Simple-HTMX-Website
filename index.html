All you need to know about
Embera Tribes

The Cascades in the Embera Community of Panama is a hidden gem that offers an unforgettable experience to travelers who are willing to venture off the beaten path. Located deep in the heart of the Panama jungle, the Cascades offer a stunning natural setting that is both awe-inspiring and serene.
The journey to the Cascades is an adventure in itself. Visitors must first take a boat ride up the Chagres River, passing through dense jungle and small communities along the way. Upon arrival, travelers are greeted by friendly members of the Embera community, who serve as guides for the journey ahead.
The hike to the Cascades is not for the faint of heart, but the stunning views along the way make it well worth the effort. Visitors will pass through lush jungle, cross rivers, and climb rocky terrain before reaching the final destination.
Once at the Cascades, travelers are rewarded with a breathtaking sight - a series of waterfalls cascading down a rocky hillside. Visitors are free to swim in the crystal clear pools at the base of the falls, take in the natural beauty of the area, or simply relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings.
Overall, a trip to the Cascades in the Embera community of Panama is an unforgettable experience that offers travelers a unique glimpse into the beauty and culture of this incredible country. It is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking an adventure off the beaten path.

Wear appropriate clothing

Wearing appropriate clothing is crucial when visiting the Cascades in the Embera Community. The Embera people have a unique culture and visitors should dress respectfully to avoid offending the locals. Comfortable, lightweight clothing is recommended for the hike to the Cascades, and visitors should avoid clothing that is too revealing or inappropriate for the cultural setting. Sturdy hiking shoes are also a must, as the hike to the Cascades can be challenging and includes rocky terrain and river crossings.

It is also important to consider the climate when choosing clothing, as the jungle can be hot and humid. Overall, dressing appropriately shows respect for the Embera culture and can help visitors to feel more comfortable and prepared for the journey ahead.

Respect the local culture

Respecting the local culture is essential when visiting the Cascades in the Embera Community. The Embera people have a unique way of life, and visitors should make an effort to understand and respect their customs and traditions. This includes wearing appropriate clothing, refraining from taking photos without permission, and being mindful of the community's privacy.

Visitors should also be respectful of any cultural events or ceremonies that may take place during their stay. Taking the time to learn about the Embera culture and showing respect for their way of life can help to create a positive and meaningful experience for both visitors and locals alike. It is important to remember that we are guests in the Embera community and should act accordingly.

Bring cash

When visiting the Cascades in the Embera Community, it is important to bring cash as there are no ATMs or banks in the area. The Embera people have a strong economy based on their traditional handicrafts, and visitors may wish to purchase souvenirs or other items during their stay.

Bringing cash ensures that visitors have the means to pay for these items, as well as any services provided by the community. It is recommended to bring enough cash to cover all expenses, as credit cards are not widely accepted in the area. By bringing cash, visitors can support the local economy and have a more enjoyable and hassle-free experience at the Cascades.

Be prepared for a challenging hike

The hike to the Cascades in the Embera Community can be challenging, and visitors should be prepared for the journey ahead. The trail can be steep and rocky in places, and there are several river crossings that require balance and care. Visitors should wear appropriate clothing and sturdy hiking shoes, and bring a backpack with essentials such as water, snacks, and sunscreen. It is also recommended to bring a walking stick or trekking poles to aid in balance and stability.

While the hike can be challenging, it offers stunning views of the jungle and the opportunity to learn about the Embera culture and way of life. By being prepared and taking the necessary precautions, visitors can safely and enjoyably experience the beauty of the Cascades in the Embera Community.
